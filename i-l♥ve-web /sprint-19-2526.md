# We ♥ Web – Talk by Bram Duvigneau

> Bram talked about things like self-driving taxis, Korean startups, robot guide dogs, smart glasses, and braille displays.

## Quote from Bram
> Code was never the hardest part of your job.

## Topics he mentioned

- There is plenty of work in tech
- Accessibility
- Creating new things (being innovative)

Bram used to be a **backend developer**, but later he moved more into **accessibility**, especially the theoretical side.  
He wants to make an impact on things that actually matter, and that’s why he focuses on accessibility.

## Thinking about your future

He said it's good to ask yourself questions like:

- What is this?
- What can I do with it?
- Where do I want to go?

As a student or graduate, you're basically trying to figure out **where you want to be**.

## Tools, AI, and assumptions

He showed an example of a **tool (like a robot assistant)** that can guide you through an app or website.

Sometimes people have assumptions about tools or AI:
- We think something will work perfectly.
- But in reality it's **yes and no**.

AI can help with some things, but it also has limitations.

For example:
- AI **doesn't have intuition**
- Humans can think creatively and understand context

## What we can bring as graduatesO

As graduates or junior developers, we can bring **fresh, creative, and insightful ideas** to a workplace or to our own projects.

This is something AI cannot really do on its own without human input.

## An interesting quote

> An LLM is trained on what already existed.  
> You are trained on what you have experienced.

## Important thing to do

> Always test with a screen reader.

### Examples of accessibility tools

- **Braille displays** (devices where you can read braille from a screen)
- **VoiceOver / screen readers** (software that reads the screen out loud)

## Questions to ask yourself when building something

When working on a website or app, think about:

- What does this mean?
- What do I actually see here?
- What does it really do?

And most importantly:

Think about **how your work is experienced by other people**.  
Because in the end, it's about how people experience your work.
